File C02 · Line of Argument

WHY WERE 272 WORDS ENOUGH?

Edward Everett spoke for more than two hours.

Lincoln had been invited to offer only “a few appropriate remarks.”

272 words.

And yet history remembered Lincoln.

Why were 272 words enough?

Lincoln reduced complex ideas to their core and followed a clear line of argument. Each section has a precise place. Each thought leads to the next.

The analysts were trying to understand the same contradiction. Their recovered records may reveal how a few remarks became a speech that endures.

Line of Argument

WHAT IS A LINE OF ARGUMENT?

A line of argument is not simply a list of what a speaker says. It describes how ideas develop and connect with one another to build the overall argument.
