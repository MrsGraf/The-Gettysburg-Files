THE WAR CHANGES

On January 1, 1863, Lincoln's Emancipation Proclamation declared enslaved people in the rebelling states to be free. Preserving the Union remained central, but emancipation was now explicitly part of the Union war effort.

File A04 · Case Reconstruction

THE CEMETERY DEDICATION

NOVEMBER 19, 1863
GETTYSBURG, PENNSYLVANIA

The records now converge: Civil War, Gettysburg, a national cemetery and invited speakers. Reconstruct the full historical situation and determine why the dedication of the Soldiers' National Cemetery, to which Lincoln had been invited, became an important moment in American history.

Gettysburg was more than a battlefield. Months after the fighting had ended, Americans gathered there on November 19, 1863 to honour the dead and dedicate a new national cemetery. Speakers had been invited to address the crowd as part of the ceremony. One of their speeches would become known as the Gettysburg Address — a speech that went down in American history.
