November 19, 1863.

Gettysburg, Pennsylvania.

A nation divided.

More than 50,000 casualties.

A cemetery is about to be dedicated.

Over the past months, historians assembled a comprehensive digital case file on one of the defining moments in American history — to explore what happened at Gettysburg and why it has shaped the American story to this day.

Hundreds of documents were collected, examined and connected: photographs, maps, eyewitness accounts, letters and official records.

Then, during the final digital transfer, large parts of the analytical archive became corrupted.

The historical sources survived.The research did not.Only fragments remain.

The archive can still be restored.

But every missing section has to be reconstructed from the surviving evidence before the complete case file can be recovered.
