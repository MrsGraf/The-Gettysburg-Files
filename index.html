Edward Everett's political career

Everett served in the U.S. House of Representatives, as Governor of Massachusetts, U.S. Minister to Great Britain, U.S. Secretary of State and U.S. Senator. He was also president of Harvard College.

What was the Emancipation Proclamation?

On January 1, 1863, Lincoln declared enslaved people in the states rebelling against the Union to be free. The proclamation did not abolish slavery everywhere in the United States, but it made emancipation a central part of the Union war effort.

CONFEDERACY

The Confederate States of America was formed in 1861 by eleven Southern states that seceded from the United States. Its government existed until 1865. The protection of slavery was central to the secession movement and to the new Confederacy.

After its defeat, the Confederacy disappeared as a state, but its memory remained politically and culturally influential. The Lost Cause narrative romanticised the Confederate cause and downplayed slavery as a central cause of the conflict. This interpretation shaped public memory, monuments and Confederate symbolism for decades and continued to influence debates into the modern era.
